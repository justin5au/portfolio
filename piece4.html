Branding nom.

Om nom nom.


nom. is an Asian fusion restaurant. We do what we can only call Drunk Asian. Comfort food served to share. Rolling Indie synthrock. Loud customers pushing plates around. One bite at a time.

As its creative director, my job was to design the restaurant's logo, various collaterals, such as menus and print advertisements, and build a campaign to create hype for its opening on the Yale University campus.

I based my design in visualizing the internet-popularized sound for eating, "nom", and infused in it an modern Asian aesthetic. I branded the restaurant with basic geometric shapes, a distinctive bite mark as a brand element, and a simple color palette of white, red and black as a homage to traditional japanese street cuisine.

To engage college students in the restaurant brand, I created a series of "nomsters", delivered in a set of collectible coasters with the distinctive nom. bite mark. These were distributed across campus, drawing participants to the restaurant's website.
